## 1. Motivation

GELATO (arXiv:2605.08384, "Geometry-preserving Embeddings via Locked Aligned Towers") demonstrates that preserving the geometry of frozen backbones yields competitive multimodal embeddings with minimal training (0.35% of weights). This principle — minimize unnecessary perturbation to well-functioning internal structure — applies to steering as well.

Interfaze (interfaze.ai/blog) proposes a specialized architecture where deterministic index-based retrieval complements a generative LLM. This aligns with the broader architectural insight: not all reliability must come from the generator itself; some should live in external specialized structure.

A good gate / risk signal / intervention should:
- **On answerable samples**: cause minimal hidden-state drift (preserve correct behavior)
- **On unanswerable samples**: cause sufficient drift to suppress hallucination

## 5. Interpretation

The oracle gate achieves the best geometry preservation on answerable samples (rel_drift=0.0000) while maintaining effective intervention on unanswerable samples (rel_drift=0.2652, ratio=265228718519.2x).
This sets the upper bound: a perfect gate knows exactly when not to perturb.
Among risk-gated modes, **entropy_gate** best preserves geometry on answerable samples.

## 6. Implications for Gate Design

The geometric perturbation metric adds a new dimension to gate evaluation:

| Traditional metrics | Geometric metric |
|---|---|
| H, C, UA (behavioral) | hidden-state drift (structural) |
| Measures what the model does | Measures what happens inside the model |
| Output-level | Representation-level |

A good gate should be evaluated on BOTH:
1. Behavioral: reduces H while preserving C (traditional)
2. Structural: minimizes drift on answerable, maximizes on unanswerable (geometric)

## 7. Verdict & Architectural Implications

**IC4_M4_DRIFT_GEOMETRY**: oracle_gate sets the theoretical upper bound (rel_drift(A)=0.0000). Among risk-gated modes, **entropy_gate** achieves the best geometry preservation (rel_drift(A)=0.0505, vs open_loop rel_drift(A)=0.1926).

**Key Finding**: All risk-gated modes show U/A drift ratios &lt; 2x. This means token-level risk signals (entropy, maxprob) do NOT provide sufficient separation between answerable and unanswerable states at the hidden-state level. The model's internal representation does not diverge strongly enough even when facing genuinely unanswerable questions.

**Implication**: Simple token-level risk signals are insufficient for selective intervention. The path forward requires either:
1. **Trajectory-level signals** (not token-local) — the model's uncertainty may only manifest across multiple tokens
2. **Internal feature directions** — rather than behavior-level proxies, use interpretability to find genuine uncertainty directions
3. **External specialized structure** — as suggested by Interfaze, not all reliability must come from the generator itself
