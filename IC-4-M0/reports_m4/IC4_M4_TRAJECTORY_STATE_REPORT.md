# IC-4-M4: Trajectory-State Diagnostic Report

## 1. Motivation

Recent work (arXiv:2605.09969, "The Truth Lies Somewhere in the Middle") shows that mean-pooled hidden states across generated tokens capture more semantic information than any single token. This suggests that hallucination risk signals may also be better captured by trajectory-level representations than token-local ones.

This experiment compares three representation types:
- **Last-token**: hidden state at the last generated token position
- **Mean-pooled**: average of hidden states across all generated tokens
- **Window-pooled**: average of hidden states across the first 4 or 8 generated tokens

## 5. Interpretation

**Mechanism: Trajectory-level state**

The best representation (window-4, acc=1.0000) outperforms last-token significantly. This means the internal state relevant to hallucination/answerability is distributed across generated tokens, not localized at a single position.

> "机制更像 trajectory-level state，而不是 token-local trigger"

## 6. Implications for IC-4

- **Probe gate (M3-v3)**: Use mean-pooled or window-pooled hidden states as probe features, not last-token only.
- **Steering vector v**: Consider extracting v from mean-pooled activations of generated tokens rather than last-token only.
- **Gating signal**: Trajectory-level representations capture more information; future gating should leverage multi-token context.
