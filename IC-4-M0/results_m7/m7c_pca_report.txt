## 4. Interpretation (REVISED)
  Full-space SNR: 0.8529
  
  ★ COUNTER-HYPOTHESIS: SNR PEAKS at PC1 (1.08), then DECREASES with more dims.
    The signal IS concentrated. The problem is NOT dimensionality.
    
  ★ REAL CAUSE of ADD-vs-REPLACE gap: WITHIN-CLASS VARIANCE
    ADD:  hs_i + diff = mean_non + (hs_i - mean_syc)  → preserves noise
    REPLACE: hs_i → mean_non                          → eliminates noise
    Each sycophantic sample has unique noise around the mean.
    ADD shifts the mean but preserves the noise → samples still "look sycophantic".
    REPLACE collapses all samples to the same state → behavior flips.
    
  ★ IMPLICATION: Control requires VARIANCE COLLAPSE, not just mean-shift.
    This explains why single-direction steering is fundamentally insufficient —
    even though the class difference IS concentrated in PC1.
    
  ★ WHY DOES THIS MATTER FOR IC-5?
    A deployable control method must either:
    (a) Learn a denoising map: hs_syc → hs_nonsyc (removes within-class variance)
    (b) Collapse the sycophancy subspace by training the model to have less variance
    LoRA adapter or anti-sycophancy fine-tuning can achieve (a) or (b).
