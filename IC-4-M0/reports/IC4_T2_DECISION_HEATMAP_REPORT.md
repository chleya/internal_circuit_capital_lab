## 6. Representation / Readout Caveat

> This experiment uses `state_last` (last-token hidden state) and
> `state_window4` (mean of last 4 positions during prefill) as probe inputs.
> These readout choices constrain what the probes can detect:
>
> 1. **Position bias**: If a behavior signal is concentrated in non-last
>    positions (e.g., question tokens), `state_last` may miss it entirely.
>    `state_window4` partially addresses this during prefill but is identical
>    to `state_last` during decode steps (only 1 position available).
>
> 2. **Probe capacity**: Logistic regression is a linear probe. If the
>    behavior signal is nonlinearly encoded, the probe will underreport
>    readability. High accuracy implies linear separability; low accuracy
>    does NOT imply absence of signal — only absence of *linearly readable*
>    signal.
>
> 3. **Causal vs correlational**: A position being readable does not mean
>    it is causally involved in generating the behavior. It may reflect
>    downstream consequences rather than upstream decisions. Steering
>    experiments (T3) are needed to establish causality.
>
> 4. **Sample size**: With small sample sizes, probe accuracy estimates
>    have high variance. Cross-validation helps but does not eliminate
>    this concern. Results should be treated as suggestive, not definitive.
